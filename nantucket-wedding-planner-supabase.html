Engagement to Honeymoon

Master Planning Timeline

Every milestone from the moment you said yes to the last sip of champagne.

Engagement
Immediately
Celebrate & Announce
  • Share the news with immediate family first
  • Post social media announcement (optional)
  • Get ring sized and insured
  • Take engagement photos
Weeks 1–4
The First Decisions
  • Set a rough budget range ($150k–$250k)
  • Agree on guest count (120)
  • Choose region: Nantucket (or similar New England)
  • Decide on season / month window
  • Begin researching wedding planners / coordinators
Month 1–2
Hire Your Planner
  • Interview 3–5 full-service wedding planners
  • Look for Nantucket / New England coastal specialists
  • Sign contract & pay retainer
  • Set up shared planning platform (this document!)
12 Months Out
12 Months Before
Lock the Venue & Date
  • Tour Galley Beach, The Wauwinet, White Elephant, private estates
  • Confirm ceremony + reception spaces (same or separate)
  • Book all event venues for all 4 days
  • Reserve hotel room block for guests
  • Set final wedding date
11–12 Months
Anchor Vendors
  • Book photographer & videographer
  • Book live band for reception
  • Book string quartet / jazz trio for ceremony & cocktail hour
  • Send save-the-dates (especially important for Nantucket — guests need to book ferries & accommodations early)
10–11 Months
Begin Vendor Conversations
  • Meet with 3+ florists; request proposals
  • Research caterers (if venue requires external)
  • Begin bridal gown search (allow 6–8 months for custom)
  • Research honeymoon destinations; speak to travel agent
9 Months Out
9 Months
Design & Stationery
  • Book florist — finalize design direction
  • Hire stationer for custom invitations
  • Finalize color palette and mood boards per event
  • Book hair & makeup artist (for full wedding weekend)
  • Begin groom's attire research
8–9 Months
Engagement Party
  • Host engagement party (~30–50 close friends & family)
  • Book rehearsal dinner venue
  • Begin building the guest list in detail
6 Months Out
6 Months
Send Invitations
  • Mail formal invitations (earlier than usual for destination)
  • Include ferry & accommodation info with invites
  • Finalize catering menus for all events
  • Book transportation for the weekend (shuttles, vintage cars)
  • First bridal gown fitting
5 Months
Honeymoon Planning
  • Book flights and hotels for honeymoon
  • Apply / renew passports if needed
  • Confirm wedding night accommodations
4 Months
Rentals & Custom Items
  • Order custom rental furniture, linens, china, glassware
  • Commission custom stationery (menus, place cards, escort cards, signage)
  • Begin DIY projects (start early — allow extra time)
  • RSVP deadline (request responses)
3 Months → Week Of
3 Months
Finalise Everything
  • Finalize guest count & seating charts
  • Final florals walkthrough with florist
  • Create full vendor schedule & day-of timeline
  • Final dress fitting
  • Send welcome bag contents to hotel
1 Month
Final Confirmations
  • Confirm all vendors — send final timeline
  • Prepare tip envelopes (cash/check for each vendor)
  • Confirm ferry reservations for guests needing assistance
  • Final hair & makeup trial run
  • Break in wedding shoes
1 Week Out
Wedding Week
  • Arrive on island early (Wednesday); planner does venue walkthrough
  • Deliver florals, rentals, and custom items to venues
  • Rehearsal on Thursday afternoon
  • Confirm headcounts with all caterers
  • Prepare emergency kit (steamer, safety pins, stain remover, medications, extra hosiery)
After the Wedding
Post-Wedding
  • Return or donate florals / decor
  • Send thank-you notes within 3 months
  • Preserve wedding gown
  • Submit marriage license
  • Coordinate name change (if applicable)
  • Select and order wedding album
  • Depart for honeymoon!

The Looks — Wedding Day
Her — The Dress
Timeless Bridal
Silhouette Column, A-line, or ballgown — personally chosen. Bias-cut silk or structured organza. Nothing trendy; something heirloom-quality. Fabric Silk charmeuse, duchess satin, mikado, or chantilly lace. No sequins. Details Consider covered buttons down the back; slight train; open back or structured bodice. After Party Change A second look — chic mini dress or a tailored separates set in ivory or champagne.
Him — The Suit
Custom Tailored
Suit Custom or made-to-measure two- or three-piece suit. Navy, midnight, or charcoal. Worsted wool or hopsack for summer. Shirt Bespoke white poplin; French cuffs with simple cufflinks. Tie Silk tie in deep navy, sage, or seafoam — or a knit tie for texture. Shoes Cap-toe Oxford or whole-cut derby; black or dark brown. Details Simple silk pocket square. No boutonnière or a single stem — greenery, ranunculus, or white garden rose. Tailors to Consider Leffot, Suit Supply (made-to-measure), Tom Ford, or local bespoke tailor.

Wedding Party — His
Navy Suits
Suit Navy suit (rented or owned) — no tuxedos, no matching looks. Shirt White dress shirt; open collar (no tie needed). Shoes Brown or tan leather loafer or oxford. Details Matching pocket square in linen ivory or sage.

Venue & Setting

  • Outdoor ceremony — waterfront bluff, garden, or beachfront
  • Options: Galley Beach, private estate lawn, Wauwinet lawn, Brant Point area
  • Weather backup: tent with sides or indoor transfer plan

Ceremony Structure

  • String quartet plays during guest seating (20 min prelude)
  • Processional: wedding party, then bride
  • Custom vows — personal and written by couple
  • One reading by a close friend or family member
  • Ring exchange — use heirloom or custom-designed rings
  • Duration: 30–35 minutes

Ceremony Decor

  • Floral arch / altar arrangement — white garden roses, ranunculus, pampas, eucalyptus; lush and abundant
  • Aisle lined with low arrangements in bud vases or lanterns
  • Petals scattered down aisle (dried or fresh white)
  • Chairs: white or natural wood cross-back — no covers
  • Colour palette: all white + ivory + green. Zero colour.

Music

  • Prelude: Debussy, Satie, Chopin — string quartet
  • Processional: Canon in D or a personally meaningful classical piece
  • Recessional: Uplifting classical — Vivaldi Spring, or a joyful pop arrangement

Nantucket Cost Considerations

Why Nantucket Costs More

  • Everything is shipped to the island — food, flowers, equipment add 15–25% vs mainland
  • Limited vendor availability drives premium pricing (book 12–18 months out)
  • Ferry logistics mean longer setup/breakdown windows (vendor overtime)
  • Peak summer season (July–August) adds 20–30% over shoulder season
  • September is optimal: good weather, slightly lower pricing, still beautiful

Where to Invest vs Save

  • Invest: Saturday photography, florals, and live band — these create lasting memories
  • Invest: Venue with waterfront views — the Nantucket setting is the design
  • Save: Friday dinner doesn't need to match Saturday in scale
  • Save: Welcome drinks can be simple — guests just want to connect
  • Save: Sunday brunch is meant to be casual — keep it light
  • Note: A full-service Nantucket wedding coordinator is non-negotiable — the island logistics require it
